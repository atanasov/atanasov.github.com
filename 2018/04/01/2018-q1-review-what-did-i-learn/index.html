2018 - Q1 Review - what did I learn

Atanas Atanasov's Blog
2018-04-01

I am using the most of my free time to learn and improve my skills I took dozens of Udemy courses about VueJS, Vuex, ES6, NodeJS. etc. I also found many good YouTube channels with IT tutorials. I am member of CodeCourse.com, which is a really good site with excelent video tutorials on many topics and technologies. My email is full with different discounts and promotions for all kind of courses in IT field. I get easily distracted from all YouTube videos, email campaigns, notifications. During the years I collected a lot of IT content, I spent some money on programming course thinking that if I do it I will be forced to finish it learn something new. But the truth is I completed maybe 30-40% of the courses. I need additional motivation, maybe spending money on something is not enough for me.
During the Q1 of the year I did good job in learning VueJS. I started to like frontend coding and component based programming. Tooling in the latest JS coding environment is crazy. Tons of libraries, packages, helping packages, different JS versions which needs to get integrated in same app. Tons of errors, and many different log files. But I am okay with all that. Just first step to embrace with JS world was the hardest. And during the years I learned to read others’ code and getting deeply into frameworks, libraries and packages to find out their logic. For Q2 I am taking another course for AI, its going to be long course. I have been thinking a lot about this topic, should I learn it, is it going to be useful anytime in my life. In the end I booked it, paid and I think it will improve my software engineer skills. Apart of web programming, I have some mobile and desktop experience. AI is not just learning another language or library like Go, Dart, ReactJs, NativeScript. AI will give me another layer or stack of skills (math, stats, bigdata). Apart of IT languages and skills I am learning some human speaking languages. I learn Mandarin and German. This also can be managed by machine learning so I can use my computer to improve my memory and language speaking skills. The conclusion is that IT is huge topic now, too many options and paths to persue career. I think it’s better for software engineers to spread their skills into different IT areas( frontend, backend, mobile, QA, AI, devops) , instead of different programming languages or libraries in same area( PHP, Perl,Python, Java, ASP.NET, NodeJS, Go). We cannot be perfect in every area, but we will have the skills and essential knowledge to know how the system works and this will allow us to do better job in our (smaller area backend). Many people say FullStack developers don’t exist. But there are many people who work on these positions and do the job. Also knowing many fields of IT world, is good for transitions and career change. PS: I keep paying for this domain and hope that someday in future it will be good and useful blog. I believe it will improve my writing skills and better expressing my ideas.
